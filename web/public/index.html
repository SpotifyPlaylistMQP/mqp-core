Let's Start with the Basics


There's no doubt that music streaming services have forever changed the way we listen to and enjoy our music. The days of purchasing physical copies of albums are dead, as the general public has access to millions of songs at their fingertips via platforms like Spotify and LastFM. As these streaming services become more prevalent and competitive, it's important that they cater towards their audiences by addressing one of the most criticized drawbacks of streaming: finding new music to enjoy.

Services like Spotify have detailed recommender systems already in place utilizing state-of-the-art approaches like matrix factorization and collaborative filtering for recommending songs based on user preferences.
