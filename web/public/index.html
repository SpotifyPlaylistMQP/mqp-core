Let's Start with the Basics


There's no doubt that music streaming services have forever changed the way we listen to and enjoy our music. The days of purchasing physical copies of albums are dead, as the general public has access to millions of songs at their fingertips via platforms like Spotify and LastFM. As these streaming services become more prevalent and competitive, it's important that they cater towards their audiences by addressing one of the most criticized drawbacks of streaming: finding new music to enjoy.

Services like Spotify have detailed recommender systems already in place utilizing state-of-the-art approaches like matrix factorization and collaborative filtering for recommending songs based on user preferences. Below, we'll walk you through how we went about optimizing our system along with performing a demo on an example playlist.


The Data



Collaborative Filtering




  • Compares the opinions and tastes between User A and User B (playlists) to predict which items (songs) a User may prefer
  • Has the capacity to focus more heavily on either users (user-based CF) or items (item-based CF)
  • Works effectively with larger datasets
  • Less practical for our goal

Matrix Factorization





  • Focuses on decomposing a standard user-item interaction matrix into the product of two lower-dimensional matrices
  • Used to discover "latent features" - which represent hidden similarities between users and items that normal collaborative filtering would not be able to achieve
  • Fast and efficient when dealing with very large datasets - no clustering necessary!
  • More practical for our goal
